Common Laboratory Tests

Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Measures components of blood (red cells, white cells, hemoglobin, platelets) to assess overall health.

Cost: KES 500–1,200

Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)

Tests for electrolytes and kidney function.

Cost: KES 1,000–2,500

Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)

Similar to BMP but includes liver function tests.

Cost: KES 2,500–3,500

Lipid Panel

Measures cholesterol and triglyceride levels to assess heart health.

Cost: KES 1,500–3,000

Thyroid Function Test (TFT)

Tests levels of thyroid hormones to diagnose thyroid disorders.

Cost: KES 1,500–3,000

Hemoglobin A1c

Measures average blood sugar levels over the past 2-3 months.

Cost: KES 1,200–2,500

Prothrombin Time (PT/INR)

Evaluates blood clotting, especially for patients on blood thinners.

Cost: KES 800–2,000

Urinalysis

Examines urine for infection, kidney health, and metabolic issues.

Cost: KES 300–800

Liver Function Tests (LFTs)

Measures enzymes and bilirubin to assess liver function.

Cost: KES 1,500–3,000

C-reactive Protein (CRP)

Detects inflammation in the body, often used for autoimmune diseases.

Cost: KES 1,000–2,500

Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)

Measures inflammation levels in the body.

Cost: KES 500–1,500

Blood Culture

Detects bacteria or fungi in the blood, often used in serious infections.

Cost: KES 3,000–5,000

D-dimer

Helps rule out blood clot conditions like deep vein thrombosis (DVT).

Cost: KES 1,200–2,500

Hepatitis Panel

Tests for hepatitis A, B, and C markers.

Cost: KES 2,500–4,000

HIV Antibody Test

Detects antibodies for HIV, used for diagnosis and screening.

Cost: KES 200–500
